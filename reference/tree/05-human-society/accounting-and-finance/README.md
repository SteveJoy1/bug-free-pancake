# Accounting and Finance

Specialist subtree under Part 05 (Human Society). Follows the same pattern as `07-technology/computing/`: a broad-audience `accounting-fundamentals.yaml` anchored in the basics, plus focused files for professional subdomains (`us-gaap.yaml`, future `ifrs.yaml`, future `audit-and-assurance.yaml`).

## Files

| File | Scope | Status |
|------|-------|--------|
| `accounting-fundamentals.yaml` | double-entry, financial statements, accrual vs cash, cost, revenue recognition — concepts an informed non-specialist should recognize. | populated |
| `us-gaap.yaml` | US-specific: FASB ASC structure, revenue recognition (ASC 606), leases (ASC 842), goodwill, etc. | populated |
| *(future)* `ifrs.yaml` | International Financial Reporting Standards — comparable scope to us-gaap. | unpopulated |
| *(future)* `audit-and-assurance.yaml` | engagement types, materiality, audit risk model. | unpopulated |
| *(future)* `financial-analysis.yaml` | ratios, DuPont, valuation approaches. | unpopulated |

## Sourcing

- Fundamentals: standard intermediate-accounting textbooks (Kieso, Weygandt).
- US GAAP specifics: **FASB Accounting Standards Codification** — the official structure (https://asc.fasb.org/). References cite ASC topic numbers where they exist.
- AICPA Uniform CPA Exam Blueprint for pedagogical breakdown of topic weights and levels.
- IFRS content, when populated, will cite **IFRS Foundation standards** (IAS/IFRS numbers).

## Claims framing

Accounting is a rule-governed field where exact citations matter. Where a fact depends on jurisdiction or standard (e.g., LIFO allowed under US GAAP but not IFRS), the distinction is explicit in the leaf. Where a principle is contested in the profession, the leaf notes that rather than asserting a single answer.
