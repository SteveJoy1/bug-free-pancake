# Running — intensity distribution and interval structure

## Summary

My working framework: **polarized / pyramidal distribution with most running easy below LT1 and a small amount truly hard** is what the endurance-training literature supports for non-elite amateurs. A **4×4-minute VO₂max session** and **6:00-on/1:00-off cruise intervals at threshold** target different systems and are not substitutes for each other — 4x4 for VO₂max / top-end, cruise intervals for threshold / durability.

## Evidence

- I described my actual week (1 HIIT, 2× 40–60 min zone-2 runs, 1× 2-hour long run below zone 2, ~100 mi/month) and asked whether it aligned with "best research." I framed the question in terms of structure, not just pace — suggesting I already knew intensity distribution was the relevant lens.
- I knew the **4×4** format specifically (Norwegian-style VO₂max protocol) and was using it weekly.
- I distinguished **cruise intervals** as a separate construct — asked if 6:00/1:00 was "HIIT-style but longer to build durability near VO₂max" — which is conceptually close to what the literature calls threshold/cruise work.
- I pushed back when the assistant claimed a "10–30 sec/mi faster than 5K pace" rule for 4×4 reps: demanded support, and the assistant walked it back to ~0–15 s/mi per Daniels. I flagged that as an unsourced rule-of-thumb worth catching.
- I framed my own pace data correctly: 6:40 for 4-min reps but couldn't hold 6:50 for a 5K. Asked whether those should have a fixed ratio — correctly identifying my intuition as a heuristic that may not hold.
- I use a 5-zone model (not the 3-zone LT model), and caught the terminology divergence when the assistant used the 3-zone framing.

## Gaps to close

1. **Is my Zone 2 actually easy?** Under the 5-zone model "Zone 2" maps to below-LT1 easy aerobic — but only if I'm honest about the HR/RPE cap. If I'm drifting into between-thresholds work I'm loading the unproductive middle.
2. **Second hard day — HIIT or threshold?** For a fit non-elite runner stuck at 1 quality day/week, adding a **threshold** session typically has better return-per-recovery-cost than adding a second VO₂max day.
3. **Pace-ratio heuristics are not reliable.** 4×4 rep pace vs 5K pace ratio is not fixed; variability in 5K conditions (heat, pacing, tapering) affects observed ratios more than training theory.
4. **Improvement expectations.** Dialed-in 4×4 blocks → roughly 0.3–1.0% per week improvement in 3K–5K pace over ~6–8 weeks; week-to-week is not monotone.
