## Open gaps

### Classical mechanics

- **Momentum vs. kinetic energy in collisions** — conflated the two on an inelastic-collision problem. Momentum is conserved in all collisions; KE only in elastic ones. See [[momentum]].
- **Negative work** — work can be negative when the force has a component opposite to displacement (friction, gravity on rising object). See [[work-physics]].
- **Noether's theorem** — not yet engaged; can't state it in plain language. See [[noethers-theorem]].
- **F = dp/dt generality** — haven't engaged with why this form is more general than F = m·a (variable mass, relativistic). See [[momentum]].
- **Net force from opposing forces** — haven't applied a = F_net/m to a simple opposing-forces problem. See [[force]].

### Fitness / endurance

- **RIR calibration** — self-reported RIR is noisy; deliberate 0-RIR sets periodically are a cheap calibration check. See [[hypertrophy-rir-and-volume]].
- **Zone 2 verification** — my 5-zone "Zone 2" is only low-intensity if I actually keep HR/RPE below LT1. Drifting into the between-thresholds middle would load the unproductive middle. See [[running-intensity-distribution]].
- **Osmotic biology** — what keeps water in cells if not glycogen? Intracellular K⁺, phosphates, proteins, and active Na⁺/K⁺-ATPase volume regulation. See [[glycogen-water-in-endurance]].
- **Protein ~25 g MPS heuristic** is for any high-quality intact protein, not whey specifically. Whole-egg tracer work established the original ~20 g ceiling. See [[protein-dose-and-mps]].

### Personal finance (tax & property)

- **Form 2210 line references**: prior-year safe harbor uses 1040 line 22 (tax after credits) as its starting point, not line 24 (total tax). Schedule 2 adjustments then apply. See [[estimated-tax-safe-harbor]].
- **Withholding is ratable** for 2210 purposes (25%/25%/25%/25% across quarters) unless Form 2210 Part II box D is checked — late-year withholding trues up penalty cleanly; late estimated payments do not. See [[estimated-tax-safe-harbor]].
- **FL property tax valuation types**: Just/Market (Jan 1 mass-appraisal) vs Assessed (Just minus SOH cap / portability) vs Taxable (Assessed minus exemptions). Portability reduces Assessed, not Just/Market. See [[fl-property-tax-portability]].
- **Corporate assessment negotiation pre-filing** has no direct residential analog — residential pre-TRIM engagement is evidence-driven (record errors, Jan 1 condition, pre-Jan-1 comps). See [[fl-property-tax-portability]].

### HVAC / thermodynamics

- **Water's heat of vaporization (~2.38 MJ/kg)** is why a portable dehumidifier adds meaningful room heat beyond its motor input — the phase change releases ~1.45 kWh of latent heat per 1 kWh of dehumidifier electricity. See [[dehumidifier-ac-thermodynamics]].
- **Net penalty is ~1/COP of dehumidifier kWh** under like-for-like comparison (shifted from AC latent to AC sensible duty), not ~60–80%. See [[dehumidifier-ac-thermodynamics]].

### Personal (Duke FL solar)

- **COG-1 wholesale rate** is what leftover kWh credits cash out at year-end — much lower than retail, so unused credits are a real loss. See [[duke-solar-net-metering]].
- **$30 minimum bill** test excludes the EV charging credit, so the EV credit cannot be used to avoid the minimum. See [[duke-solar-net-metering]].
