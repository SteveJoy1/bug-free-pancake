# Work (Physics)

## Summary

Answered "Nope." to whether work can be negative. It can — work is a **signed scalar**, positive when force has a component along displacement, negative when opposite.

## Gaps to close

1. **W = F · d · cos θ**. When 90° < θ ≤ 180°, cos θ < 0, so W < 0.
2. **Everyday examples of negative work**:
   - Friction on a sliding box — opposes motion, does negative work (removes KE).
   - Gravity on a ball moving upward — force down, displacement up → negative work.
   - Brakes on a moving car — opposes motion.
3. **Why it matters**: the work-energy theorem (W_net = ΔKE) needs the sign — otherwise you can't explain why things slow down.
